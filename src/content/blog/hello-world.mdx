I debated for a while whether to write an introductory post at all. Every blog starts with one and most of them read the same way: *"Hi, I'm [name], welcome to my corner of the internet, I'll be writing about [things]."* Then the author posts twice and disappears.

I'll try to do it differently. No promises on posting frequency. No content calendar. Just honest writing, when I have something worth saying.

## Who I am

I'm Enrico — a software developer based in a small town in northern Italy, currently studying Computer Science at the University of Padua while working on a handful of ambitious independent projects. Professionally I've spent two years across backend, mobile and frontend development, mostly in the .NET and Apple ecosystem.

The way I approach problems tends to be research-first: I'd rather spend a day understanding something properly than a week cleaning up a misunderstanding. That mindset shapes most of what I build — and most of what ends up making me write.

## What you'll find here

Honestly, a mix.

**Development.** The thing I spend most of my time on. Architecture decisions, tools I like or don't, things I've learned the hard way, opinions on how software should be built. Not tutorials — I have little interest in writing yet another "how to set up a React project". More like thinking out loud about craft.

**Projects.** I'm building several things in parallel right now — a .NET library ecosystem, an agri-food supply chain platform, a consumer product I can't name yet. When there's something worth reporting: progress, pivots, design choices, lessons learned — I'll write about it here.

**Music.** It's a big part of my life outside the screen and I'll write about it occasionally. What I'm listening to, what I think about it, why it matters. Expect no genre loyalty.

**Thoughts.** The category I'll probably use most and define least. Things that annoy me, ideas I can't stop thinking about, observations that don't fit anywhere else. Some of these will be fully formed arguments. Some will be half-finished and I'll publish them anyway because that's what blogs are for.

## A note on language

I'm Italian. Everything on this site — posts, documentation, project pages — is written in English, and that's a deliberate choice: I want my thinking to be accessible to anyone, and I want people to feel free to open a discussion, leave a comment on a project, or just reach out, regardless of where they're from.

That said, if you're Italian and want to write to me in Italian, go ahead. No issue with that at all.

## What this isn't

A newsletter. A personal brand. A content strategy.

I'm not writing to build an audience. I'm writing because it forces me to think more clearly, and because some of what I figure out might be useful to someone else. If you find something here worth reading, great. If not, that's fine too.

The portfolio and CV sections of this site are the professional face. This is something else.
